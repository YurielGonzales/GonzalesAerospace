Space Craft

Sputnik 1 was a small, spherical spacecraft measuring about 58 centimeters (23 inches)
in diameter and weighing around 83.6 kilograms (184 pounds). It was equipped with four
whip-like antennas and transmitted radio signals, which could be detected on Earth.
The satellite's main objective was to orbit the Earth and gather information about the density of the
upper atmosphere and the propagation of radio signals in space.The propulsion system of Sputnik 1
consisted of a modified R-7 intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM), known as the "Semiorka."
The R-7 ICBM was one of the most powerful rockets developed during that time and played a crucial role
in launching the satellite into space. The launch vehicle provided the
initial thrust to propel Sputnik 1 into orbit around the Earth.With a top speed of around 29,000MPH.

Apollo 11 was the historic spaceflight mission that successfully landed the first humans on the Moon.
Launched by NASA on July 16, 1969, the Apollo 11 mission marked a significant milestone
in human exploration and represented the culmination of years of scientific and technological development.
The crew of Apollo 11 consisted of three astronauts: Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins.
Armstrong, as the mission commander, became the first person to set foot on the lunar surface,
with Aldrin following shortly after. Collins, the command module pilot,
remained in lunar orbit aboard the Command Module Columbia. With a top speed of around 25,000MPH.

Starship is a next-generation spacecraft being developed by SpaceX, the aerospace company founded by Elon Musk.
Designed for a wide range of missions, including crewed space travel, satellite deployment, lunar and Mars missions,
and point-to-point travel on Earth, Starship represents a significant leap forward in space transportation
technology.Starship features a stainless steel construction, which provides strength and durability while withstanding
the extreme conditions of space travel. The spacecraft has a distinctive conical shape with a wide base and a sleek,
aerodynamic design. It stands approximately 50 meters (165 feet) tall and is capable
of carrying a substantial payload, including both cargo and passengers.With an estimated top speed of 25,000MPH.
