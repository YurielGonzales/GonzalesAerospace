Planes

The Messerschmitt Me 262, also known as the Schwalbe (German for "Swallow")
or Sturmvogel (German for "Stormbird"), was a revolutionary jet-powered fighter aircraft developed by Germany during World War II.
It was the world's first operational jet-powered fighter aircraft and had a significant impact on the future of aviation.
The Me 262 was designed by the German engineer, Hans von Ohain, and his team at the Ernst Heinkel Company.
It featured a sleek, streamlined fuselage and swept-back wings, which gave it a distinctive appearance.
The aircraft was powered by two Junkers Jumo 004 axial-flow turbojet engines mounted under each wing.
Reaching a top speed of 540MPH.

The Mikoyan-Gurevich MiG-15, commonly known as the MiG-15,
was a jet fighter aircraft developed by the Soviet Union during the early stages of the Cold War.
It became one of the most widely produced
and successful jet fighters of its time and had a significant impact on aerial warfare.The MiG-15 was designed by Artem Mikoyan and
Mikhail Gurevich at the Mikoyan-Gurevich Design Bureau. It featured a swept-wing design,
which offered improved high-speed performance and maneuverability.
The aircraft was powered by a single Klimov VK-1 centrifugal-flow turbojet engine.
And has a top speed of 668MPH or Mach 0.91 (1 Mach is the speed of sound).

The Airbus A320 is a popular and highly successful narrow-body commercial aircraft developed by
Airbus Industrie, a European aerospace manufacturer. Introduced in the late 1980s,
the A320 family of aircraft has become a cornerstone of the global aviation industry, revolutionizing short-
to medium-range air travel.The A320 was designed to
compete with the Boeing 737 series and incorporated several innovative features.
It introduced fly-by-wire technology, where electronic signals replace traditional mechanical controls,
providing precise and efficient aircraft control. This technology improved safety,
reduced pilot workload, and enhanced overall flight performance.
With a top speed of 561MPH.

The F-35 Lightning II, commonly known as the F-35, is a fifth-generation
multirole fighter aircraft developed by Lockheed Martin for the United States and its international partners.
At the heart of the F-35's impressive performance is its advanced propulsion system, which includes the Pratt & Whitney F135
turbofan engine.The F135 engine is a high-performance powerplant specifically designed for the unique demands of the F-35.
It is a two-spool engine that combines advanced technologies to deliver exceptional thrust, efficiency, and operational flexibility.
The engine is capable of providing up to 43,000 pounds of thrust
, enabling the F-35 to achieve supersonic speeds and excellent maneuverability. With a top speed of ~1200MPH or Mach 1.6
